A brief history of my career

About me!

For the last decade I have been involved in the electronics side of the security industry building and repairing PCBs, assembling industial grade security cameras and in my current position providing support to other field/installation technicians, operators and getting involved with the design and QC side of the buisness.

Below is a brief history of how I started my career with Vision Intelligence (previously ATF Vision),

Assembler and installer

Here was the starting point of my career and ultimately where I found my passion for electronics. I was the first full time employee starting at the company and my main role was to soley build cameras, simply put I would just assemble them like lego when in the warehouse and when complete install them where needed.

2014-2015

Electronics technician

Fast forwarding a bit and the company grew exponentially, our original team of 4 grew to a team of 8, with branches starting in each state. My team/branch was the central hub for buisness, my role would be to train the new Assemblers and be in control of shipping the complete cameras to the other states while primarily installation and maintenence focused.

Here the company allowed me to travel Australia installing cameras for our biggest customers, A notable experiance was installing temporary cameras in Amberly air base having to be escorted by 3 other personnel and having my phone and laptop confiscated.

2015-2017

Technical support officer

Travelling forward again the company continues to grow rapidly, I get promoted to TSO which gets me more involved in the programming and technical side of the buisness. At this point my work has me primarily using Linux systems - mostly Ubunutu and Linux Mint.

2017-2020

Senior Technical Officer

in my current role I work almost almost exclusively work with the Electronics engineers testing new products, different designs and hardware upgrades while being a point of escalation for the TSO's who are unable to narrow down faults.

2020-current
